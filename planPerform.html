Purpose

Planning and Performance Measures provides support and assistance to operational processes by coordinating strategic planning and performance measures, as well as designing and administering surveys.

Activities

Planning and Performance Measures consists of three functional areas: Strategic Planning, Performance Measures, and Customer Survey Administration.

Strategic planning assistance begins by coordinating and facilitating meetings; documenting strategic goals, objectives, and strategies; and providing performance information and research to support key initiatives. The process ends with the development of key strategic initiatives and project updates, which are reported monthly. The strategic planning process also plays a vital role in providing information for and preparation of the program’s portion of the Department’s annual Long-Range Program Plan (LRPP).

The performance measures process begins by reviewing prior year actual performance results. After factors affecting actual performance, if any, are considered, revisions to the performance standard may be recommended. Preparation of budget amendments requesting performance measure changes may ensue. Performance accountability measures (PAMs) and enforced collections data are collected, analyzed, monitored, and reported monthly to senior leadership. The PAMs report is incorporated annually into the LRPP. Other reports tracked and reported on a quarterly basis are: Revenue Performance Report and the Department of Economic Opportunity (DEO) Strategic Information Report.

The process of creating a survey instrument begins with a request and a determination of the specific data or information needed to address the issue. The survey questions and response sets are defined, and the prospective respondents and method of survey administration (online or paper) are selected. Once a timeline for administration is established, the survey is piloted and administered. The process ends with an actionable report of data and information.
