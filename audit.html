Purpose

Audit verifies the accuracy of tax and information reported on returns, notifies filers of any errors and corrective action required, identifies non-registered taxpayers, and resolves unagreed assessments.

  

Activities

The process of auditing begins with the examination of both registered and nonregistered taxpayers' accounting records to determine compliance with tax laws. Examinations may be conducted at the taxpayer's place of business or through correspondence. The taxpayer may appoint a representative, generally a CPA or an attorney, to work with the auditor and represent them during the audit. For sales and use tax, a taxpayer may request a certified audit examination, which is conducted by an independent CPA and paid for by the taxpayer. During the examination, the auditor may review hard copy records or records maintained in an electronic environment. The auditor also reviews the appropriate statutes, rules and technical materials governing the tax being audited. The auditor informs the taxpayer of the findings, including additional liabilities due or overpayment made, and provides the appropriate work papers and other documentation supporting the audit findings. Throughout the audit process auditors educate taxpayers about future compliance. The process ends when the audit case file is closed and protest rights have expired, or a protest is filed.
