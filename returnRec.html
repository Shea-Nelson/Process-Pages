Purpose

Return Reconciliation verifies the accuracy and intent of taxpayer-filed return data to ensure an accurate distribution and taxpayer filing history and amends and corrects previously filed returns. Additionally, Return Reconciliation processes and posts electronic and paper payments returned by the issuing bank through the state Treasury for all taxes.

Activities

The process of reconciling a return begins with the loading of the return into the math audit subroutine. The subroutine is a series of edits that audits the four corners of the return. The subroutine can make automated changes to a return based on the way it was originally filed. After review by the subroutine, correctly filed returns go directly into the filing history of the taxpayer. Returns that do not meet the edits generate exceptions, which are reviewed within Return Reconciliation. The taxpayer information is reviewed to determine if data was accurately captured. The review may also involve contacting the taxpayer for additional information and reviewing the taxpayer’s filing history and registration data. Return Reconciliation also identifies and timely notifies registered taxpayers of their failure to file an accurate and/or timely return, and informs them of corrective action required (e.g., additional liabilities owed, taxes overpaid, or instructions to file a required return) to ensure compliance with tax laws.

The Returned Items Unit provides a copy of the original check in the form of an image replacement document (IRD), and makes account corrections and adjustments. Work is received through debit memos issued by Treasury and requests from service centers. The TAMS Unit receives requests from Service Centers and Central Operations to amend or correct a previously filed tax return. Requests are received and reviewed by Regional SUNTAX Representatives (RSRs) and forwarded to the TAMS Unit. The process ends when, either by subroutine or manual review, a return is complete and loaded into history; a returned item received is posted to the taxpayer’s account; or a TAMS request to amend or correct a return is completed.
