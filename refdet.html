Purpose

Refund Determination timely credits or refunds tax overpayments. The process is mandated by statute and delegated to the Department of Revenue by the State's Chief Financial Officer.

Activities

The refund determination process begins when there is an overpayment of tax which must be refunded to the taxpayer. During this process, a refund audit of the refund claim and supporting documentation is performed. The process ends when a determination is made to approve or deny the refund claim. Refund audits may be generated by a claim from a taxpayer, an overpayment made on a return, or an audit of a taxpayer's records resulting in overpayment of tax. The process also provides oversight to the activity by which reemployment tax refunds are approved. For approved refunds, this process ends when the refund audit is completed by the auditor, properly reviewed by the Refund Review Section, and posted to the applicant’s account. For refunds being denied or withdrawn, the process ends when a determination is made, based on documentation received from the applicant, to deny or withdraw the refund claim.
