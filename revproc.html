Purpose

Revenue Processing deposits paper payments into the State Treasury, as mandated by state law to fund governmental operations. Primary external customers are the Treasury, other state agencies and local governments. Additionally, Revenue Processing prepares physical documents for conversion to TIF files to capture data from paper tax returns and other documents, to make documents available to users, and to archive documents for future reference. GTA processes are the primary internal customers.

Activities

Revenue Processing begins with the receipt of checks and associated payment coupons or tax documents. Documents received without a check are sent directly to the Imaging Center for imaging and data capture. Documents must first be prepared for conversion to TIF files so data can be captured, and the documents can be made available to users and archived for future reference. All documents are inspected and prepared for imaging. Staples and paperclips are removed, tears are repaired, and non-standard size documents are taped to standard size pages. Checks and tax documents are sorted by tax and document type, then forwarded to Screening. Screening ensures payment coupons have the appropriate information needed for posting to the correct account, tax, and applied period. Screeners batch like-remittances and print web header sheets for each batch. Validations runs the remittance coupons and checks through high-speed equipment that images the front and the back of the documents. Documents are physically retained until the recording of payments is completed in the Return Reconciliation Process. Following completion of the reconciling process, physical coupons and documents are destroyed, and the images of them constitute the document of record. Using image recognition software and/or manual data entry, the information associated with each payment is perfected. A payment file is generated and sent to SUNTAX for posting to taxpayer accounts. When this file has been successfully uploaded, a confirmation is received from SUNTAX, which automatically starts a process to prepare a file containing the deposit data and images of the checks for transmission to the bank. The file is then electronically transmitted to the bank for deposit. The checks are stored for 30 days after the electronic file has been transmitted, then destroyed and the process ends.
