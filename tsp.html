Purpose

Taxpayer Services responds to tax filers, taxpayers, or taxpayer representatives when they request tax information. The Taxpayer Services contact center receives inbound calls and correspondence requesting tax information as well as inquiries regarding collection, registration, e-assistance and other issues.




Goals

Achieve an overall answer rate of 90%

Answer 80% of calls within 20 seconds

Obtain first contact resolution rate of 90%

Improve upon customer satisfaction benchmark of 82%

Activities

The activities performed by Taxpayer Services crosses multiple business process areas, e.g., Taxpayer Aid and Assistance, Receivables Management and Account Management. The process of providing service to tax filers, taxpayers, or taxpayer representatives begins when an inquiry is received over the telephone or in writing through email or postal mail. Taxpayer Services agents respond to taxpayers who have contacted the Department concerning tax law questions. Agents also respond to inquiries regarding bills and delinquency notices issued for all taxes administered by the Department. These notices are the result of the taxpayerâ€™s failure to file a return, pay the full amount of a tax liability, or file and pay timely; or when the taxpayer has made an error on their return, which requires correction. Taxpayer Services seeks to resolve all issues upon first contact, including preventing accounts from escalating to the service center for further collection and enforcement activity.

The number of calls received, agent and self-serve answer rate, and overall answer rate are tracked and reports compiled. Customer satisfaction information, such as caller wait time and survey results on customer satisfaction and first contact resolution, is also tracked for reporting purposes. The process ends when the taxpayer receives a response to their inquiry.
