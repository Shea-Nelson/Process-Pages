Purpose

Account Creation establishes taxpayer and employer accounts for entities required to file tax returns and/or remit taxes, entities exempt from paying tax, and other entities required to have a relationship with the Department.

Activities

The process of creating an account begins with the receipt of information that identifies an entity as required to file tax returns, pay taxes, or is exempt from taxation. The primary source of information is taxpayers who voluntarily register with the Department either electronically or by using a paper form.   Account Creation also receives information from initial tax return filings from unregistered taxpayers or from other Department processes. During the account creation process, the data captured includes business entity name, address, phone number, entity type (corporation, partnership, etc.), as well as the business type and the taxes for which a liability exists. Each newly-registered entity is assigned a unique business partner number that is shared by all taxes for which the entity is required to file. The process ends when all required data has been accurately entered, the account is properly created and obligated for tax (if applicable), and the account information is viewable or electronically retrievable.
