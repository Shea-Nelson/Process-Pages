Purpose

Revenue Accounting, also referred to as Fund Distribution/Reconciliation, disburses tax revenues received by the Department to the appropriate governmental agency. These distributions fund governmental operations and programs, both statewide and locally. Before any distribution is performed, a review of the depository information is completed to ensure revenue is posted to the appropriate funds.

Activities

The revenue accounting process begins with the reconciliation of all receipts and disbursements to the various tax funds within GTA. This ensures all transactions are properly recorded in both the Department’s accounting system and the State Comptroller’s records. Revenue Accounting performs distributions to both State agencies and local governments. Once a distribution is complete, the payment information to the local governments is posted on the Revenue Confirmation website to give the local governments additional payment information.

Revenue Accounting’s distribution of funds is the end process for all financial processes within the Department of Revenue. When a distribution is performed, the previous month’s revenue and audit adjustments, as well as the current month’s refunds and any other adjustment performed on the taxpayer accounts are all included in the distribution calculations.

Revenue Accounting also maintains the Clerk of Court Revenue Remittance System, Medicaid Reimbursement Remittance System, Red Light Camera Remittance System, the Registration Information Sharing and Exchange Program (RISE), Municipal Public Service Tax database and the Revenue Confirmation website.
