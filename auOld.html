Purpose

The purpose of the Field Audit Process is to verify the accuracy of information reported on tax returns, to notify tax filers of errors and any corrective action required, and to educate tax filers to ensure compliance with Florida tax laws.

Goals

To ensure compliance in a fair and equitable manner for all Florida businesses through the efficient review and closing of audit cases within statutory guidelines.

Activities

The audit process begins when notification is made to a taxpayer that an examination of books and records will be undertaken to verify the accuracy of the information reported on the tax return. The examination includes the review of books and records by visiting the taxpayerâ€™s location and through written correspondence. The auditor consults appropriate statutes, rules and technical materials governing the tax under examination. The taxpayer is informed of the audit findings, this includes additional liabilities due or overpayment made, and is provided with appropriate work papers and/or other documentation supporting audit findings. Throughout the audit process we educate the taxpayer on future compliance.
